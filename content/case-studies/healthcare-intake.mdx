## The problem

New-patient intake was the network's most painful non-clinical workflow. Front-office staff spent ~40 minutes per new patient: phone tag, paper forms, insurance verification by fax, chart creation by hand.

## How we scoped it

Two weeks shadowing intake. We mapped the actual steps (vs. the documented ones — they'd drifted apart) and identified the four decision points that required a human versus the eighteen that didn't.

## What we built

A conversational intake agent reachable by web, SMS, or phone. It qualifies the patient, verifies insurance eligibility against the network's payer APIs, books into the scheduling system, and produces a ready-to-review chart for front-office approval.

## Compliance

The entire agent runs in the client's HIPAA-compliant AWS account. No patient data leaves that boundary. Model weights are hosted alongside — no external LLM call. Our engagement passed the client's security review and a third-party HIPAA audit during the measurement window.

## What the team owns now

The agent, the infrastructure, the retraining pipeline, and the audit logs. Ongoing support is optional — the client's internal engineering team took over at month 4.
